
Confusion over the term Ruthenian. In the 11-12th century, the term had two meanings. The term denoted the Rus state, which after the Mongol invasion in 1240, survived only on the territory of Volhynia (Volyn) and Galicia (Halych) until 1349. The term also referred to an ethnicity made up of earlier tribes of Severians and Polans. By the 13th century, the ethnicity of the Ruthenians included the Volhynians and White Croats. By the 13th-14th century, the term has another meaning - Orthodox person. In the meaning of an Orthodox person, the term extends to the territory of modern Belarus and part of Russia. Subsequently, the term changed its meanings many more times and had several meanings at the same time.
